Approach/retraction - CYCLE79

Sequence for plane-by-plane machining

  1. The tool approaches the starting point at rapid traverse at the height of the retraction plane and is fed in to the safety clearance.

  2. The tool traverses the multi-edge in a quadrant at machining feedrate. The tool first traverses to the machining depth and then moves in the plane. The multiple edge is machined depending on the programmed machining direction (up-cut/down-cut) in a clockwise or counterclockwise direction.

  3. When the first plane has been machined, the tool retracts from the contour in a quadrant and then infeed to the next machining depth is performed.

  4. The multi-edge is traversed again in a quadrant. This process is repeated until the depth of the multiple edge has been reached.

  5. The tool retracts to the safety clearance with rapid traverse.

    Note

    A multi-edge with more than two edges is traversed helically; with a single or double edge, each edge is machined separately.

Sequence for helical machining

  1. The tool approaches the starting point at rapid traverse at the height of the retraction plane and is fed in to the safety clearance.

  2. The starting point for machining is then approached at machining feedrate. The multi-edge is then traveled round on an oblique path until the depth of the multi-edge is reached. At the programmed final depth, the contour is traveled round once completely to remove the residual material.

    The multiple edge is machined depending on the programmed machining direction (up-cut/down-cut) in a clockwise or counterclockwise direction.

  3. The tool then exits the contour on a quadrant path.

  4. The tool retracts to the safety clearance with rapid traverse.

    If multiple plane infeeds are necessary, this operation is repeated.

    Note

    With a single or double edge, each edge is machined separately and in oscillating movements.
