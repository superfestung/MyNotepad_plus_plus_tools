Machining - POCKET3

Machining type

  • Roughing

    • Plane-by-plane

      When roughing, the individual planes of the rectangular pocket are machined one after the other from the center point until depth Z1 is reached.

    • Helical

      The shapes of the rectangular pocket can be square, rectangular, in the form of a groove or round. Depending on the pocket shape, not all insertion strategies are suitable. If the pocket is not square or round, then only oscillating insertion strategies can be used. After insertion, the tool is removed from the hole. Starting from this, milling is carried out on inclined (helical) paths from the reference point of the pocket (top) down to the pocket depth. In the plane, corresponding to the plane infeed, infeed is realized from the inside to the outside.

  • Finishing

    • Plane-by-plane

      During finishing, the edge is always machined first. The rectangular pocket edge is approached on the quadrant that joins the corner radius. In the last infeed, the base is finished from the center out.

    • Helical
      When finishing, the outer wall is machined helically downwards and then the base is machined from the outside to the inside in a spiral.

  • Edge finishing

    • Plane-by-plane

      Edge finishing is performed in the same way as finishing, except that the last infeed (finish base) is omitted.

    • Helical

      Only the outer pocket wall is helically finished.

  • Chamfering

    Chamfering involves edge breaking at the upper edge of the rectangular pocket.

    Geometries when chamfering inside contours

    Note

    The tip angle is evaluated when chamfering. During chamfering, the end mill behaves like a centering tool with a 90° tip angle.

    Note

    The following error messages can occur when chamfering inside contours:

    • Safety clearance in the program header too large
      This error message appears when chamfering would, in principle, be possible with the parameters entered for FS and ZFS, but the safety clearance then could not be maintained.

    • Immersion depth too large
      This error message appears when chamfering would be possible through the reduction of the immersion depth ZFS.

    • Tool diameter too large
      This error message appears when the tool would already damage the edges during insertion. In this case, the chamfer FS must be reduced.
