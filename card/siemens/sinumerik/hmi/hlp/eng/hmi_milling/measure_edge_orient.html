Edge measurement

The following options are available to you when measuring an edge:

Align edge

The workpiece lies in any direction, i.e. not parallel to the coordinate system on the work table. By measuring two points on the workpiece reference edge that you have selected, you determine the angle to the coordinate system.

Distance 2 edges

The workpiece lies parallel to the coordinate system on the work table. You measure distance L of two parallel workpiece edges in one of the axes (X, Y, or Z) and determine its center.

Requirement

You can insert any tool in the spindle for scratching when measuring the workpiece zero manually.

- OR -

An electronic workpiece probe is inserted in the spindle and activated when measuring the workpiece zero automatically.

Procedure

 

1.

Select the "Machine" operating area and press the <JOG> key.

 

2.

Press the "Workpiece zero" softkey.

 

3.

Press the "Align edge" softkey.

 

 

- OR -

 

 

Press the "Distance between 2 edges" softkey.

 

 

- OR -

 

 

If these softkeys are not listed, press any vertical softkey (with the exception of "Set edge") and select the desired measurement variant in the drop-down list.

 

4.

Select "Measuring only" if you only want to display the measured values.

 

 

- OR -

 

5.

In the selection box, select the desired work offset in which you want to store the zero point.

 

 

- OR -

 

 

Press the "Select work offs." softkey to select an settable work offset.

In the window "Work offset – G54 ... G599", select a work offset, in which the zero point should be saved and press the "In manual" softkey.

You return to the measurement window.

 

6.

Distance 2 edges:

Under Meas. dir. P1, select the desired measuring axis in which you want to approach the workpiece and the measuring direction (+ or -) for points of measurement P1 and P2.

Align edge:

Under "Measuring axis", select the axis in which you want to approach the workpiece, and the measuring direction (+ or -).

 

7.

Align edge:

If the measuring axis is the tool axis, also select a reference axis in which "P2" is offset to measure the point.

If the measuring axis is an axis of the plane, no selection is required because the other axis of the plane is automatically the reference axis.

 

8.

Align edge:

After you have set up the rotary axes on the machine and selected a work offset for the correction in step 5, select between offset into a rotation of the coordinate system and into the shift of a rotary axis in the "Angle offs." field.

 

9.

Distance 2 edges:

Enter the reference point (setpoint) for the center of the distance between the 2 edges in the measuring axis.

Align edge:

Enter the setpoint angle between reference axis and workpiece edge. The setpoint angle indicates the desired angle between the positive direction of the reference axis and the edge of the workpiece.

If the setpoint angle=0, the edge is aligned parallel to the reference axis after correction.

 

10.

Traverse the tool to the workpiece edge.

 

11.

Press the "Save P1" softkey.

 

12.

Reposition the tool and repeat the measuring procedure to measure the second point, and then press the "Save P2" softkey.

 

13.

Press the "Calculate" softkey.

Distance 2 edges

The length or the distance between the 2 edges as well as the center point in the measuring axis between the 2 edges are calculated and displayed.

Align edge

The angle between the workpiece edge and reference axis is calculated and displayed.

 

 

- OR -

 

 

Press the "Set ZO" softkey.

Distance 2 edges

With "Set WO" the center point in the measuring axis between the 2 edges now corresponds to the selected reference point. The calculated shift is stored in the translation of the work offset.

Align edge

With "Set ZO", the workpiece edge now corresponds to the setpoint angle.

The calculated angle is stored in the rotation of the coordinate system of the work offset or in the shift of a rotary axis. When correcting for the shift of the rotary axis, the actual position of the rotary axis is taken into account in addition to the angle between reference axis and workpiece edge and the setpoint angle. The correction value is calculated so that the alignment of the edge to the setpoint angle can be achieved by positioning the rotary axis at 0 degrees.

Note

Settable work offsets

The labeling of the softkeys for the settable work offsets varies, i.e. the settable work offsets configured on the machine are displayed (examples: G54…G57, G54…G505, G54…G599).

Please observe the information provided by the machine manufacturer.

Automatic measurement

 

1.

Prepare the measurement (see steps 1 to 9 above).

 

2.

Traverse the workpiece probe close to the workpiece edge on which you wish to measure and press the <CYCLE START> key.

 

 

This starts the automatic measuring process. The position of measuring point 1 is measured and stored.

The "P1 stored" softkey becomes active.

 

3.

Repeat the operation to measure and store P2.

 

4.

Press the "Calculate" softkey.

Distance 2 edges

The length or the distance between the 2 edges as well as the center point in the measuring axis between the 2 edges are calculated and displayed.

Align edge

The angle between the workpiece edge and reference axis is calculated and displayed.

 

 

- OR -

 

 

Press the "Set ZO" softkey.

Distance 2 edges

With "Set WO" the center point in the measuring axis between the 2 edges now corresponds to the selected reference point. The calculated shift is stored in the translation of the work offset.

Align edge

With "Set ZO", the workpiece edge now corresponds to the setpoint angle.

The calculated angle is stored in the rotation of the coordinate system of the work offset or in the shift of a rotary axis. When correcting for the shift of the rotary axis, the actual position of the rotary axis is taken into account in addition to the angle between reference axis and workpiece edge and the setpoint angle. The correction value is calculated so that the alignment of the edge to the setpoint angle can be achieved by positioning the rotary axis at 0 degrees.

Align the edge by positioning the rotary axis

After you have performed the angle correction of the workpiece edge in the shift of a rotary axis, the edge of the workpiece can be aligned to the selected setpoint angle by positioning the rotary axis. The rotary axis is positioned to the position of 0 degrees.

Note

Retract the probe to a safe position before the rotary axis is due to move.

 

 

The activation window asking whether you want to "Position rotary axis X to align?" is open.

 

1.

Select "Yes" if you want to position the rotary axis.

An input field for the feedrate and the softkey "Rapid traverse" are displayed.

 

2.

Press the "Rapid traverse" softkey to enter the feedrate in rapid traverse.

 

 

- OR -

 

 

Enter the desired feedrate into input field "F".

 

3.

Press the <CYCLE START> key.

The rotary axis is repositioned.

Aligning the edge by swiveling

If a swivel data record (tool carrier) is active, you can align the edge to the setpoint angle by swiveling. The basic position of the swivel data record is approached.

 

 

The activation window with the query "Align edge?" is opened.

 

1.

Select "Yes" if you want to align the edge by swiveling to the basic position.

If it has been defined in the swivel data record, corresponding prompts appear for retracting before swiveling, the preferred direction for swiveling, and tracking of the tool tip when swiveling.

Answer the queries.

 

2.

Select the relevant parameters.

 

3.

Press the <CYCLE START> key.

After the axes have been retracted, the edge of the workpiece is aligned with the setpoint angle by swiveling into the basic position.
