Machining - CYCLE77

You can select the machining mode for milling the circular spigot as follows:

  • Roughing

    Roughing involves moving round the circular spigot until the programmed finishing allowance has been reached.

  • Finishing

    If you have programmed a finishing allowance, the circular spigot is moved around until depth Z1 is reached.

  • Chamfering

    Chamfering involves edge breaking at the upper edge of the circular spigot.

    Note

    During chamfering, the end mill behaves like a centering tool with a 90° tip angle.

For "roughing" and "finishing" machining, it is possible to choose between machining types "plane-by-plane" and "helical".

  • Plane-by-plane
    The circular spigot is reached plane by plane with a constant depth until the programmed final depth is reached.

  • Helical
    In helical machining, the machining is performed on an oblique path. At the programmed final depth, the contour is traveled round completely to remove the residual material.
