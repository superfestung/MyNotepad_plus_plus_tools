Machining - CYCLE76

  • Roughing

    Roughing involves moving around the rectangular spigot until the programmed finishing allowance has been reached.

  • Finishing

    If you have programmed a finishing allowance, the rectangular spigot is moved around until depth Z1 is reached.

  • Chamfering

    Chamfering involves edge breaking at the upper edge of the rectangular spigot.

    Note

    During chamfering, the end mill behaves like a centering tool with a 90° tip angle.

For "roughing" and "finishing" machining, it is possible to choose between machining types "plane-by-plane" and "helical":

  • Plane-by-plane
    The rectangular spigot is reached plane by plane with a constant depth until the programmed final depth is reached.

  • Helical
    For helical machining, the number of edges must be greater than 2. In helical machining, the machining is performed on an oblique path. At the programmed final depth, the contour is traveled round completely to remove the residual material.
