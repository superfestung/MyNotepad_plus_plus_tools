Approach/retraction

  1. The tool approaches the center point of the semicircle at the end of the slot at rapid traverse at the height of the retraction plane and adjusts to the safety clearance.

  2. Then, the tool enters the workpiece at machining infeed (taking into consideration the maximum infeed in the Z direction and the finishing allowance). The circumferential slot is machined in the programmed machining direction (up-cut or down-cut) in a clockwise or counterclockwise direction.

  3. When the first circumferential slot is finished, the tool moves to the retraction plane at rapid traverse.

  4. The next circumferential slot is approached along a straight line or circular path and then machined.

  5. The tool moves back to the safety clearance at rapid traverse.
