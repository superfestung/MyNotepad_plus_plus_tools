Arrange signals in tracks

The "Arrangement in tracks" function can be used in the toolbar to display signals of the trace diagram in separate tracks. Only the visible signals are arranged.

The binary signals are positioned in a group at the lower end of the trace diagram and their height is set to a pre-configured value.

The non-binary signals are positioned above all the binary signals and their height is adjusted so that they fill the remaining space.

If there are only binary signals or only non-binary signals, the height of the signal tracks is adjusted to fill the available space in the trace diagram.

When the signal curves are rearranged, the signal names in the legend widget are also automatically re-arranged, so that their sequence matches the order of signal curves in the trace diagram.

Each signal is arranged separately, even if the signals are grouped. As a result, the minimum or maximum vertical range values for signals in the same group is inconsistent. You can resolve this by entering a new minimum of maximum limit into the signal table.

An error message is output if the vertical size of the trace diagram is not sufficient to display all signals with the configured minimum heights and clearances for the tracks.
