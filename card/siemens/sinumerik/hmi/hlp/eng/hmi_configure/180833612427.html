Undo zoom and scroll actions

To restore the previous visible ranges of the signal curves in the trace chart after performing a zoom or scroll action, click the "undo zoom" icon on the toolbar. The following actions can be undone:

  • Zooming or scrolling with mouse wheel, touch functions, scrollbars of the trace chart, keyboard combinations or by drawing a rectangle

  • Moving the trace diagram or individual signal curves by dragging and dropping;

  • Using the zoom functions from the toolbar or from the context menu;

  • Arranging signals in tracks

  • Scrolling the trace chart as a result of moving a measurement cursor to the edge of the visible range

  • Changing the minimum or maximum Y values of signals in the signal table

  • Automatic change of the visible range as a result of modifying the bit mask of a signal.

You can undo a maximum of five actions. An action can be, for example, a single click on a zoom / scroll function button or context menu item. When you perform continuous actions, such as:

  • Moving the trace diagram by dragging and dropping

  • Continuously scrolling by keeping the mouse button pressed on a scroll bar

The action has been completed when you release the mouse button.

When you scroll the trace view by repeatedly pressing keyboard shortcuts in quick succession, or when you zoom in / out using the mouse wheel, the action is finished when the visible ranges of the signals do not change for at least 2 seconds.

If you scroll the trace diagram by moving a measurement cursor, the "undo" function restores only the visible ranges of the signals. The positions of the cursors do not change.

The "undo" history is cleared when you perform any of the following actions:

  • Add a new signal to the configuration

  • Modify signal scaling groups

  • Start a new trace recording

  • Create a new trace session.

Deleting a signal from the configuration does not clear the "undo" history.
