Machining with main spindle and counterspindle

For instance, a new blank can be clamped in the main spindle, and a blank that has already been machined at the front can be clamped in the counterspindle. With the ShopTurn program, initially the workpiece is machined in the main spindle, and then the rear side of the workpiece, already machined at the front, is machined in the counter spindle.

Note

Various workpieces

You have the option of machining two different workpieces at the main spindle and counterspindle.
