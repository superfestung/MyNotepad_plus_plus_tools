Calibrate radius at edge

When electronic probes are inserted into the spindle, clamping tolerances usually occur. This can lead to measurement errors.

In addition, you need to determine the trigger points of the probe relative to the spindle center (trigger points).

Therefore, you need to calibrate the electronic probe.

The radius at the edge is calibrated at a selected axis and direction, and calibrated at a reference surface that is perpendicular to this. This can be done, for example, at a workpiece.

The determined trigger point is transferred into the addressed calibration data field.

Procedure

 

1.

Load the workpiece probe into the spindle.

 

2.

Enter the approximate radius in the tool data.

 

3.

Select the "JOG" mode in the "Machine" operating area.

 

4.

Move the workpiece probe over the reference edge.

 

5.

Press softkeys "Meas. workp." and "Probe calibration".

Window "Calibration: Probe" opens.

 

6.

Press the "Radius at edge" softkey.

The input window "Calibrate: Radius at edge" opens.

 

7.

Enter reference point Y0 of the edge, e.g. of the workpiece or the machine table.

The radius of the workpiece probe is determined.

 

8.

Press the <CYCLE START> key.

Calibration starts.
